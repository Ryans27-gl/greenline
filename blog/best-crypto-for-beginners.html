Best Crypto for Beginners: What to Buy First in 2026

5 min read · Updated April 2026

There are over 20,000 cryptocurrencies. Most of them are worthless. A few are legitimate. And two of them are where every beginner should start.

Start with Bitcoin

Bitcoin (BTC) is the original cryptocurrency and still the biggest. It has been around since 2009, survived every crash and scandal, and is now accepted by major institutions worldwide. For beginners, Bitcoin is the safest entry point — the most liquid, the most widely understood, and the least likely to go to zero.

You do not need to buy a whole Bitcoin. On most exchanges you can buy $10 worth and own a fraction. That is a perfectly reasonable way to start.

Then Consider Ethereum

Ethereum (ETH) is the second-largest cryptocurrency. While Bitcoin is mainly a store of value (like digital gold), Ethereum is more like a platform — thousands of apps, games, and financial tools are built on top of it. It is riskier than Bitcoin but has more growth potential.

A common beginner strategy is to split your first investment 50/50 between Bitcoin and Ethereum. This gives you exposure to both the safe bet and the growth bet.

What About Everything Else?

Every coin that is not Bitcoin is called an altcoin. Some are legitimate projects — Solana, Cardano, Polygon. Others are meme coins that exist purely because of internet hype. The honest truth is that most altcoins will lose value over time. Stick with Bitcoin and Ethereum until you have spent a few months learning how the market moves.

How Much Should You Start With?

Only invest what you would genuinely be okay losing. Crypto can drop 30-50% in a matter of weeks. A reasonable first investment is $10 to $50 — enough to make it feel real without risking anything meaningful. Once you are comfortable with the volatility, you can add more.

What to Avoid

  • Any coin that promises guaranteed returns. Nothing in crypto is guaranteed. Anyone who says otherwise is trying to take your money.
  • Meme coins and "100x" picks from social media. By the time you hear about it on TikTok, the early buyers have already sold.
  • Sending crypto to strangers. If someone DMs you offering help or free crypto, it is a scam. Every single time.
  • Leverage and margin trading. These amplify losses just as much as gains. Beginners should never touch them.

The One Rule That Matters

Do not invest money you need for rent, bills, or emergencies. The people who do best in crypto buy a reasonable amount, hold patiently, and do not panic when prices drop. Start small. Learn as you go. Stay informed.
