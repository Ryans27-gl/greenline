Recitals

WHEREAS, the Company operates Greenline, a financial news and market data platform; and

WHEREAS, the Company desires to engage the Contractor to provide social media management and content creation services; and

WHEREAS, the Contractor represents that it has the skills, qualifications, and experience necessary to perform such services;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements set forth herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

1. Engagement and Relationship

The Company hereby engages the Contractor, and the Contractor hereby accepts engagement, as an independent contractor to perform the services described herein. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employer-employee relationship, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the Parties. The Contractor shall not be entitled to any employee benefits, including but not limited to health insurance, retirement plans, paid leave, or workers' compensation.

The Contractor shall be solely responsible for all federal, state, and local taxes arising from compensation received under this Agreement, including self-employment taxes.

2. Scope of Work

The Contractor shall provide the following services for Greenline (the "Services"):

  1. Social Media Management: Day-to-day management and operation of Greenline's social media accounts across designated platforms, including but not limited to Instagram, X (Twitter), LinkedIn, TikTok, and Facebook;
  2. Content Creation: Development, production, and editing of original content including graphics, images, short-form video, written posts, stories, and other media suitable for social media distribution;
  3. Content Strategy: Planning and scheduling of content calendars, developing audience engagement strategies, identifying trending topics relevant to Greenline's financial news and market data niche;
  4. Account Management: Monitoring engagement metrics, responding to comments and messages in a timely manner, managing community interactions, and reporting on performance analytics;
  5. Brand Consistency: Ensuring all content aligns with Greenline's brand guidelines, voice, visual identity, and editorial standards;
  6. Additional Services: Such other related tasks as may be mutually agreed upon by the Parties in writing.

3. Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue for an initial term of ______________, unless earlier terminated in accordance with Section 9 of this Agreement. The Agreement may be renewed or extended by mutual written agreement of the Parties.

5. Ownership of Work Product

All content, materials, designs, graphics, videos, copy, strategies, and other work product created by the Contractor in the performance of the Services (collectively, "Work Product") shall be the sole and exclusive property of the Company. The Contractor hereby assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to the Work Product, including all intellectual property rights therein, including but not limited to copyrights, trademarks, and trade secrets.

The Contractor agrees to execute any documents and take any actions reasonably requested by the Company to perfect, evidence, or vest in the Company the rights assigned under this Section. This assignment shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement.

The Contractor retains no rights to use, reproduce, distribute, or display the Work Product for any purpose, including portfolio use, without the prior written consent of the Company.

6. Confidentiality

The Contractor acknowledges that in the course of performing the Services, the Contractor may have access to confidential and proprietary information of the Company, including but not limited to business plans, financial data, user data, analytics, marketing strategies, product roadmaps, and trade secrets ("Confidential Information").

The Contractor agrees to:

  1. Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;
  2. Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent of the Company;
  3. Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing the Services;
  4. Return or destroy all Confidential Information upon termination of this Agreement.

This confidentiality obligation shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of two (2) years.

7. Representations and Warranties

The Contractor represents and warrants that:

  1. All Work Product shall be original and shall not infringe upon any intellectual property rights of any third party;
  2. The Contractor has the full right, power, and authority to enter into this Agreement and to perform the Services;
  3. The Contractor shall perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards;
  4. The Contractor shall comply with all applicable laws, rules, and regulations in the performance of the Services, including without limitation laws relating to financial content, advertising disclosures, and data privacy.

8. Indemnification

The Contractor shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Company and its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to: (a) any breach of this Agreement by the Contractor; (b) any negligent or wrongful act or omission of the Contractor; or (c) any claim that the Work Product infringes upon the intellectual property rights of any third party.

9. Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement:

  1. For Convenience: Upon fourteen (14) days' prior written notice to the other Party;
  2. For Cause: Immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within seven (7) days after receiving written notice thereof;
  3. Immediately: If the Contractor engages in conduct that is harmful to the Company's reputation, brand, or business interests.

Upon termination, the Contractor shall: (i) immediately cease all work on behalf of the Company; (ii) deliver all completed and in-progress Work Product to the Company; (iii) return or destroy all Confidential Information; and (iv) transfer all account credentials, access rights, and administrative controls for any social media accounts managed on behalf of the Company. The Company shall pay the Contractor for all Services satisfactorily performed through the date of termination.

10. Non-Compete

During the term of this Agreement and for a period of one (1) year following its termination or expiration, the Contractor shall not, directly or indirectly:

  1. Provide social media management, content creation, or similar services to any business that directly competes with Greenline in the financial news aggregation, beginner investing education, or stock market data platform space;
  2. Solicit, divert, or take away any clients, customers, users, or business relationships of the Company that the Contractor had contact with or knowledge of during the term of this Agreement.

The Contractor acknowledges that the restrictions in this Section are reasonable and necessary to protect the Company's legitimate business interests and that any breach of this Section would cause irreparable harm to the Company. This provision shall be enforceable to the maximum extent permitted under the laws of the State of Michigan.

11. Non-Solicitation

During the term of this Agreement and for a period of one (1) year thereafter, the Contractor shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, or hire any employee or contractor of the Company, or encourage any such person to leave the Company's service.

12. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Michigan, without regard to its conflict of laws principles. Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in the State of Michigan.

13. Entire Agreement and Amendments

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, warranties, commitments, and agreements, whether written or oral. This Agreement may not be amended or modified except by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

14. Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. The invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable while preserving the Parties' original intent.

15. Notices

All notices required or permitted under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed delivered when sent by email with confirmation of receipt, or when delivered personally or by certified mail to the addresses set forth above.

16. Counterparts

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid and binding.
