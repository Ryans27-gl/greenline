What is crypto?

Crypto is digital money that lives on the internet. No bank or government runs it — it's kept alive by a network of computers around the world.

How does it work?

Every transaction is written into a shared public list called a blockchain. Thousands of computers all keep the same copy, so nothing can be faked or changed after the fact.

What's a wallet?

A wallet is where your crypto lives. It holds a private key that proves you own your coins. Lose the key, lose the crypto — so most beginners start with a wallet built into their exchange.

What's an exchange?

An exchange is the app you use to buy and sell crypto. Connect your bank, deposit a few dollars, and you're ready to own your first Bitcoin. Most exchanges come with a wallet built in.

Bitcoin vs altcoins

Bitcoin was the first and is still the biggest. Everything else — Ethereum, Solana, Dogecoin, and thousands more — is called an altcoin. Think of Bitcoin as the safe bet and altcoins as the newer, riskier ones.

Getting Started on Coinbase

After you verify your identity, link a bank account or debit card. That's your gateway to buying crypto. Start by exploring the home screen — you'll see top coins, price charts, and a simple Buy button at the top.
Don't stress about picking the "right" coin on day one. Buying $5 of Bitcoin is enough to learn how everything works before you commit more.

Buying Your First Coin

Tap Buy, choose Bitcoin (or any coin you're curious about), and enter a dollar amount. You don't need to buy a whole coin — you can own $5 worth. Review the order, tap Buy now, and it's yours. Coinbase stores it in your account automatically.
Start small and add more over time. Buying a little every week is called dollar-cost averaging and it's one of the safest ways to grow your crypto.

Keeping Your Crypto Safe

Turn on two-factor authentication in your Coinbase settings — this is the single biggest thing you can do to protect your account. Once you own more than you'd be upset losing, look into moving it to your own wallet where only you hold the keys.
Never share your password, seed phrase, or login codes with anyone. If someone DMs you asking for help, it's a scam 100% of the time.

Getting Started with Robinhood Crypto

Robinhood puts crypto right next to your stocks. Open the search bar, type Bitcoin, and you'll see a price chart just like a stock. The Buy button works exactly the same way you'd buy a share of Apple or Tesla.
Robinhood only supports a handful of coins. If you want to branch out into smaller altcoins later, you'll need a dedicated exchange like Coinbase.

Placing Your First Trade

Tap the coin, tap Trade, and choose Buy. Enter a dollar amount — any amount — and tap Review. Robinhood will show you exactly how much crypto that buys. Submit the order and it's in your account within seconds.
Robinhood doesn't charge commissions but it does build the fee into the price. Keep that in mind if you plan to trade in and out often.

What to Watch For

Crypto moves fast. A coin can swing 10% in a single day, so don't panic when prices drop. Read the news, stick to coins you understand, and only invest what you're comfortable leaving alone for a while. That's how most successful holders do it.
Turn on price alerts in Robinhood for any coin you own. A quick notification is better than refreshing the app every 5 minutes.

How ChangeNOW Works

ChangeNOW doesn't ask for an account. You pick the coin you have, pick the coin you want, and paste your wallet address. In a few minutes the new coin lands in your wallet. It's fast, anonymous, and works for 900+ cryptocurrencies.
Double-check your wallet address before sending anything. Crypto transactions are final — if you send to the wrong address, there's no undo button.

Making Your First Swap

Choose how much you want to swap and ChangeNOW shows you the estimated amount you'll receive. Paste in the wallet address where you want the new coin sent, then send your original coin to the address ChangeNOW gives you. Within minutes the swap is done.
Use the fixed-rate option if you want to know exactly how much you'll get before you send. It's slightly more expensive but removes all the guesswork.

Staying Safe

Since ChangeNOW doesn't hold your crypto, the responsibility is on you to keep your wallet secure. Back up your seed phrase, store it somewhere offline, and never type it into a website. If you lose the seed phrase, the crypto is gone for good.
Write your seed phrase on paper and store it in two separate safe places. Phones and cloud storage can be hacked — paper cannot.

Crypto Basics for Any Platform

No matter which app you use, the first step is the same: link a payment method and buy a small amount of Bitcoin or Ethereum. These are the two biggest and safest coins to start with. Once you own some, the rest starts making sense quickly.
Only invest what you'd be okay losing. Crypto can go up fast, but it can also drop just as fast. Start small and grow as you get more comfortable.

What to Buy First

Most people start with Bitcoin because it's the most established and least volatile of the major coins. Ethereum is the second most popular and powers thousands of apps and projects. Stick to these two for your first few weeks and you'll avoid most of the common beginner mistakes.
Ignore any coin that promises guaranteed returns or 100x gains. Those are almost always scams. The real gains come from patience and learning.

Keeping Your Crypto Safe

Turn on two-factor authentication on every platform you use. Never share your seed phrase or password with anyone. And ignore anyone who DMs you offering help or free crypto — those are always scams. Follow those three rules and you'll avoid 95% of the problems new crypto users run into.
