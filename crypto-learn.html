What is crypto?

Cryptocurrency is digital money that exists on the internet. Unlike dollars in your bank, crypto isn't controlled by any government or bank — it's powered by a global network of computers running software that nobody owns.

Think of crypto like cash for the internet. You can send it anywhere in the world in minutes, and no one needs permission to use it.
⛓️

How does crypto work?

Every transaction is recorded on a shared public ledger called a blockchain. Instead of one bank keeping the records, thousands of computers around the world all hold the same copy — which is why it's nearly impossible to cheat or fake.

Imagine a notebook that everyone on Earth has a copy of. If you try to change yours, the other copies catch you instantly.
👛

What's a crypto wallet?

A crypto wallet is where you store your coins. It holds a secret key that proves you own your crypto. Wallets can live on your phone, in a browser extension, or on a physical device.

Your wallet isn't the crypto itself — it's more like the key to your safety deposit box. Lose the key and you lose access, so keep it safe.
🏛️

What's an exchange?

An exchange is an app or website where you can buy, sell, and trade crypto. You connect your bank account, deposit money, and use it to buy Bitcoin or other coins. Some exchanges also give you a built-in wallet so you don't have to manage your own.

An exchange is like an app store for crypto. You browse what's available, pay, and it's yours.
🌐

Bitcoin vs altcoins

Bitcoin was the first cryptocurrency and is still the biggest. Every other coin — Ethereum, Solana, Dogecoin, and thousands more — is called an "altcoin" (alternative coin). Bitcoin is seen as digital gold, while altcoins often try to do new things like run apps, process payments, or power games.

Bitcoin is like gold — slow, solid, trusted. Altcoins are like startups — riskier, but some become the next big thing.
